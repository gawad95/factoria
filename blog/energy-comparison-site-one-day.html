From idea to live affiliate site in one day: how we built Switcherr

Switcherr compares 7 French electricity providers, captures user profiles via email, and earns affiliate commissions. Zero hosting cost. Shipped in one session.

The French electricity market opened to competition in 2007, but most French households still don't know they can switch providers and save €100–500/year. That's a classic affiliate arbitrage opportunity: high-intent users searching "meilleur fournisseur électricité 2026" with commercial intent, and a comparison tool that earns a commission when they convert.

We shipped Switcherr in a single day. Here's the full breakdown.

The product decision

Affiliate comparison sites work when three conditions are met:

  1. Search volume exists for "best X in [country]"
  2. At least one provider has an affiliate program
  3. The comparison tool provides genuine utility (not just a list of links)

French electricity hits all three. Monthly searches for electricity comparison terms exceed 50,000. Papernest, Selectra, and direct provider programs all pay commissions. And the actual comparison logic — cost per kWh × annual consumption + annual subscription — is genuinely useful because most users don't do the math themselves.

The stack: zero infrastructure, zero hosting cost

The entire product is a single HTML file with Tailwind CDN and vanilla JavaScript. No framework. No build step. No server. Deployed to GitHub Pages — which is free and auto-deploys on every push.

The comparison engine

The core calculation runs entirely in the browser with no API calls:

  • User inputs annual consumption (kWh), tariff option (Base or Heures Creuses), and subscribed power (kVA)
  • The engine calculates annual cost for each provider: annual = (kwh × pricePerKwh) + subscriptionPerYear
  • Results sort by price, with savings vs. current supplier highlighted
  • Each provider card links to the provider's subscription page with UTM parameters

We priced 7 providers using March 2026 data pulled directly from each provider's tariff pages. The data is baked into the HTML — no API, no database. Updating prices takes 5 minutes and a git push.

The email capture

Price comparison users are high-intent but may not convert immediately. They shop around, compare, and come back later — often without remembering where they found the best deal.

We capture email with consumption profile attached: "Alert me when a better deal appears for my profile." The form sends to Formsubmit.co (free, zero accounts needed) with hidden fields carrying the user's consumption/option/puissance/supplier data. That gives us segmented leads for follow-up, not just a flat email list.

The SEO layer

The comparator itself is a JavaScript-rendered app — not great for SEO on its own. So we added a static blog with three posts targeting high-volume French queries:

  • "Meilleure offre électricité 2026" — informational intent, high volume
  • "Comment changer fournisseur électricité" — how-to intent, captures mid-funnel
  • "OHM vs EDF vs Ekwateur" — comparison intent, direct affiliate CTA

Each blog post ends with a CTA to the comparator with UTM tracking. Sitemap.xml submitted to Google. Estimated time to first organic traffic: 4-8 weeks.

The monetization path

Right now: direct UTM links to each provider's subscription page. No formal affiliate agreement, so no commissions yet — just traffic tracking. Next step: apply to Papernest's partner program (they aggregate and earn commission on every switch, sharing a portion with referring sites).

The lesson: You can build a fully functional affiliate site in one day and make it commission-ready in one more. The bottleneck isn't building — it's approval by the affiliate program and Google indexing. Both are in motion the moment you ship.
